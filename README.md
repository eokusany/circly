# Circly

**The app that notices before you ask.**

Circly is a mobile app for people in recovery and the people who care about them. It creates a calm, structured space where someone in recovery can share their journey on their own terms — and where supporters can show up without overstepping.

## Why we're building this

Recovery doesn't happen alone, but the tools available today either put too much pressure on the person recovering or leave supporters in the dark. Most apps treat recovery like a solo productivity problem — streak counters, habit trackers, journaling prompts — but ignore the human relationships that actually sustain long-term recovery.

On the other side, supporters (family, partners, close friends) are left guessing. They don't know when to reach out, when to give space, or how to help without hovering. The result is a painful gap: the person recovering feels surveilled or unsupported, and the supporter feels helpless or intrusive.

Circly closes that gap. It gives people in recovery full control over what they share and when, while giving supporters meaningful, guided ways to be present. The core principle is simple: **supporters can support, not supervise.**

And for the people who need help most — those who go quiet, who stop checking in, who isolate — Circly notices. The Silent Support Engine detects absence and gently nudges supporters to reach out, so no one falls through the cracks.

## What it does

### For people in recovery
- **Daily check-ins** — log how you're holding up (sober, struggling, good day) with optional private notes
- **Sobriety streak tracking** — milestone badges at 1 day, 7 days, 30 days, 90 days, and 1 year
- **Private journaling** — write entries that stay yours unless you choose to share them
- **"I'm okay" tap** — a zero-friction daily signal that lets your circle know you're alright
- **Emergency button** — instantly alert your supporters when you need help right now
- **Full privacy control** — you decide what each supporter can see

### For supporters
- **Shared updates** — see check-ins and milestones your person has chosen to share
- **Warm pings** — send a one-tap "I'm with you" message without conversation pressure
- **Silence nudges** — get notified when someone in your circle goes quiet, so you can reach out
- **Encouragement messages** — short notes of support, delivered gently
- **Emergency alerts** — immediate sound, vibration, and alert when someone needs you

### The Silent Support Engine
The feature that makes Circly different. It combines three systems into one loop:

1. **"I'm okay" Loop** — the person in recovery taps once daily to generate a liveness signal
2. **Silent Signal** — the server detects when someone stops checking in and nudges their supporters
3. **Warm Ping** — supporters respond with a one-tap presence signal that says "I'm here" without requiring a conversation

Silence becomes the signal. No one has to ask for help — the app notices and connects people automatically.

## How it works

Circly uses a **multi-context system**. When you sign up, you choose your context:

- **Recovery** — you're tracking your own journey and sharing with your circle
- **Supporter** — you're showing up for someone you care about
- **Family** — a blend of both, designed for caregiving relationships (e.g., elderly parent care)

Each context gets a tailored experience — different dashboard, different copy, different features — but everyone shares the same underlying platform.
